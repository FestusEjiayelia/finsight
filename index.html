Financial literacy changes lives
Over 40 million Nigerians have mobile wallets but no tools to understand their spending. These lessons are designed for anyone who wants to build real financial knowledge — starting today.
💰
The 50/30/20 Rule
Divide every naira you earn into three buckets: 50% for needs (rent, food, transport), 30% for wants (data, entertainment), and 20% straight into savings. This single rule, followed consistently, builds wealth over time.

Based on your data: you're spending about 98% of income on needs + wants and saving roughly 2% from surplus. Target: get savings to 20%.
Rule: Save first. Spend what's left.
📊
Understanding Your Net Worth
Net worth = everything you own minus everything you owe. Even with a small income, you can grow your net worth by reducing debt and consistently adding to savings and investments.

Your OWealth deposits show you are already building the savings habit. The next step is keeping more of what you deposit — by reducing outgoing transfers.
Rule: Assets − Liabilities = Net Worth. Grow it every month.
🛡️
Emergency Fund First
Before investing, build a fund covering 3–6 months of living expenses. This protects you from debt when unexpected things happen — job loss, health issues, emergencies.

At your avg monthly spending of ₦299k, your emergency fund target is ₦900k – ₦1.8M. With a consistent ₦20k/month set aside, you'd reach ₦900k in 45 months.
Target: 3–6 months of expenses in liquid savings.
